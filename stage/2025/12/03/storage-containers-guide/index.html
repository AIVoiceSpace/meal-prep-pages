    The Ultimate Storage & Containers Guide for Meal Preppers

    2 minute read

    Introduction: Why Good Storage Matters

    The right storage container can transform your meal prep experience. It’s not just about keeping food fresh, but also about organizing your fridge, transporting meals, and even serving sizes. This guide explores what makes a good meal prep container and dives into the best options available for every budget and lifestyle.

    What to Look For: Selection Criteria

    When choosing a meal prep container, consider the material, size, compartments, and lid type. Glass containers are durable and microwave-safe but can be heavy. Plastic containers are lightweight and affordable, but check for BPA-free versions. Consider containers with dividers for portion control, and ensure the lids are airtight for freshness.

    Real-World Setup Examples

    Example 1: The Minimalist

    A minimalist meal prepper might opt for a set of glass containers. They’re durable, easy to clean, and see-through, which makes fridge organization a breeze.

    Example 2: The Bulk Prepper

    For bulk preppers, a large set of lightweight plastic containers might be the best option. They’re easy to stack and store, and you won’t run out halfway through your meal prep.

    Example 3: The Budget Conscious

    Mason jars are a popular choice for budget-conscious preppers. They’re affordable, reusable, and perfect for a variety of meals.

    Maintenance & Longevity

    Extend the life of your containers by hand-washing when possible, storing them properly, and avoiding extreme temperature changes. Remember, investing in quality containers can save money in the long run by reducing the need for replacements.

    Community Recommendations

    The r/MealPrepSunday community loves the versatility of mason jars, the durability of glass containers, and the convenience of lightweight plastic containers. But the best container for you depends on your personal needs and meal prep style.

    Getting Started Guide

    Start with a basic set of containers that fits your budget. As you get more comfortable with meal prep, you may want to invest in higher-quality containers or specialty containers for different types of meals.
