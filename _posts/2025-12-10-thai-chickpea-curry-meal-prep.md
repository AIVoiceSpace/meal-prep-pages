## Recipe Overview

Looking for a tasty, nutritious, and budget-friendly meal prep option? Well, you're in the right place. This Quick Thai Chickpea Curry is a one-pot wonder that's as easy to prepare as it is delicious. Perfect for vegetarians or those looking to incorporate more plant-based meals into their diet, this curry offers a burst of Thai-inspired flavors that will keep your taste buds intrigued all week long.

This meal prep recipe covers your weekday lunches or dinners, and with a prep time of just under an hour, you can prepare enough servings to last you through the week. Not to mention, it's incredibly cost-effective, making it a great choice for meal preppers on a budget.

## Ingredients & Sourcing

Here's what you'll need to make this flavorful curry:

- 2 cans chickpeas, drained and rinsed
- 1 large onion, diced
- 4 cloves garlic, minced
- 2 tablespoons Thai red curry paste
- 1 can coconut milk
- 2 cups vegetable broth
- 2 tablespoons soy sauce
- 1 tablespoon lime juice
- 2 cups spinach
- Salt and pepper to taste

Most of these ingredients can be found at your local grocery store. For specialty items like the Thai red curry paste, you can check out your local Asian market or buy it online through this [CURRY_PASTE_LINK].

## Step-by-Step Prep Instructions

### Sunday Prep Phase

1. Start by gathering and washing all your ingredients. This should take about 5 minutes.
2. Next, dice your onion and mince your garlic. This will take approximately 10 minutes.

### Cooking Phase

1. In a large pot over medium heat, cook the onion until it's translucent. This should take around 5 minutes.
2. Add the garlic and curry paste, cooking for another 2 minutes until fragrant.
3. Add the chickpeas, coconut milk, vegetable broth, soy sauce, and lime juice. Bring the mixture to a boil. Once boiling, reduce the heat and let it simmer for 15 minutes.
4. After 15 minutes, add the spinach and cook until it wilts. This should only take about 2 minutes.
5. Season with salt and pepper to taste.

### Cooling & Storage

Once your curry has cooled, divide it into individual servings in airtight containers. These [GLASS_CONTAINERS_LINK] are a great option for storage. Store your curry in the fridge for up to a week.

## Variations & Swaps

If you're not vegetarian, you can add some cooked chicken or shrimp to this curry. You can also swap the chickpeas for other types of beans or lentils. If you prefer a different type of grain, feel free to substitute the rice for quinoa, couscous, or even noodles.

## Storage & Reheating

This curry stores well in the fridge for up to a week. To reheat, you can simply microwave it or warm it on the stovetop. If you prefer to freeze it, make sure to cool it completely before storing it in freezer-safe containers.
