    Breakfast Prep for the Week: Egg Muffins, Oats, and Smoothie Packs

    2 minute read

    Recipe Overview

    Does your morning routine often involve skipping breakfast or grabbing something unhealthy on the go? We’ve got your solution right here! These three breakfast meal prep strategies are all about making your mornings less stressful, and more delicious and nutritious. These recipes are perfect for a week’s worth of breakfasts and can be easily customized to your taste. The best part? Minimal cooking required!

    Ingredients & Sourcing

    Here’s a snapshot of what we’ll need:

    Egg Muffins:

    • 12 large eggs
    • 1 cup chopped vegetables (spinach, bell peppers, onions)
    • 1/2 cup shredded cheese
    • Salt and pepper

    Overnight Oats:

    • 5 cups old fashioned oats
    • 5 cups milk or plant-based milk
    • Sweeteners and toppings of choice (fresh fruits, nuts, seeds, honey)

    Smoothie Packs:

    • Fresh fruits (bananas, berries, peaches)
    • Leafy greens (spinach, kale)
    • Yogurt or plant-based milk
    • Optional: protein powder, chia seeds, flax seeds, honey

    Step-by-Step Prep Instructions

    Sunday Prep Phase

    Egg Muffins:

    1. Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and grease a muffin tin.
    2. Beat the eggs in a large bowl, season with salt and pepper.
    3. Add the chopped vegetables and cheese to the eggs, stir well.
    4. Pour the egg mixture into the muffin tin, filling each cup about 3/4 full.
    5. Bake for 20-25 minutes until the eggs are set.

    Overnight Oats:

    1. In individual jars, add a cup of oats each.
    2. Pour a cup of milk or plant-based milk into each jar.
    3. Add your preferred sweeteners and toppings.
    4. Stir well, cover, and refrigerate overnight.

    Smoothie Packs:

    1. In individual ziplock bags, add a combination of your preferred fruits and leafy greens.
    2. Add a scoop of protein powder, seeds, and sweeteners, if using.
    3. Seal the bags and store them in the freezer.

    Cooling & Storage

    Let the egg muffins cool before storing them in airtight containers. Your overnight oats are already in their individual containers, ready to grab-and-go in the morning. The smoothie packs will stay in the freezer until you’re ready to blend them up!

    Meal Prep Matrix

    Day Breakfast Additional Toppings/Add-ins
    Monday Egg Muffins Salsa, avocado
    Tuesday Overnight Oats Fresh berries, nuts
    Wednesday Smoothie Pack Chia seeds, honey
    Thursday Egg Muffins Hot sauce, Greek yogurt
    Friday Overnight Oats Banana slices, peanut butter
    Saturday Smoothie Pack Flax seeds, protein powder
    Sunday Egg Muffins Ketchup, fresh herbs

    Variations & Swaps

    Feel free to get creative with your toppings and add-ins based on your preference or dietary needs. For the egg muffins, you can add cooked bacon, ham, or switch the cheese type. In your overnight oats, try different types of milk, sweeteners, and toppings. You can also add cocoa powder for a chocolatey twist! As for the smoothie packs, the combinations are endless.

    Storage & Reheating

    All three breakfast options can be stored in the fridge for up to a week. Egg muffins can be reheated in the microwave for 30-60 seconds, while overnight oats can be eaten cold or warmed up if you prefer. The smoothie packs go straight into the blender from the freezer.

    Community Variations

    A member of r/MealPrepSunday suggested adding a scoop of Greek yogurt to the overnight oats for added creaminess and protein. Another member swaps spinach with zucchini in their egg muffins for a low-carb version.
